# Name:
    KNOT
# Why: 
    KNOT is a digital preservation ecosystem that transforms cultural heritage into connected, living stories, ensuring that future generations inherit not only the artifacts of the past, but the memories and relationships that gave them meaning. To understand and preserve any space, it must be seen through its history and context, because no past exists in isolation.
# How:
    **Context First** — Every artifact is captured with its historical, cultural, and social background, not in isolation.
    **Connected Stories** — All entries are linked through people, places, and events to form a relational network of heritage.
    **Living Archive** — Preservation is continuous, allowing stories to evolve, expand, and gain new perspectives over time.
# What:
    1. 3D Memory Worlds — Immersive spaces where heritage sites, objects, and stories are experienced spatially.
    2. Digital Heritage Archive — A structured, searchable repository of cultural artifacts and their histories.
    3. Story Maps & Timelines — Visual connections showing how people, places, and events evolve over time.
